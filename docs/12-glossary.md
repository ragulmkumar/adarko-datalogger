# Glossary

This section explains technical terms used in the ADARKO Datalogger app and documentation in simple, non-technical language.

## A

**Access Point Name (APN)**: A setting that tells your datalogger which cellular network to use for sending data. It's like choosing which internet provider your device should connect to.

**Android**: The operating system used by most smartphones and tablets. The ADARKO Datalogger app runs on Android devices.

## B

**Battery Level**: How much power remains in your datalogger's batteries. Shown as a percentage, with higher numbers meaning more power available.

**BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy)**: A type of Bluetooth that uses very little power. Used for connecting your phone to datalogger devices without draining batteries quickly.

**Bluetooth**: A wireless technology that lets devices communicate over short distances. Used by the app to connect to datalogger devices.

**Bluetooth Permissions**: Approval you give the app to use your phone's Bluetooth features. Required for the app to work.

## C

**Configuration**: The settings on your datalogger device. Includes things like server address, send interval, and network settings.

**Connect**: To establish a Bluetooth link between your phone and a datalogger device. Required before you can manage the device.

**Connection Timeout**: When the app stops trying to connect to a device after waiting too long. Usually happens if the device is out of range or not responding.

## D

**Device ID**: A unique code that identifies each datalogger device. Helps you tell different devices apart.

**Device Name**: A friendly name given to your datalogger device. Makes it easier to identify which device is which.

**Disconnect**: To end the Bluetooth connection between your phone and datalogger device.

## E

**EUI64**: A unique 64-bit number that permanently identifies your datalogger device. Like a serial number that can't be changed.

## F

**Firmware**: The software that runs inside your datalogger device. Controls how the device works and can be updated wirelessly.

**Firmware Update**: Installing new software on your datalogger device. Improves performance, adds features, and fixes problems.

**Firmware Version**: A number showing which version of software is installed on your device (e.g., "02.15").

## H

**Hardware Version**: A number showing the physical design version of your datalogger device (e.g., "01.00").

## L

**Location Permission**: Approval you give the app to know your general location. Required by Android for Bluetooth scanning (not used for tracking).

## M

**MTU (Maximum Transmission Unit)**: The maximum amount of data that can be sent at once over Bluetooth. Affects how quickly firmware updates transfer.

## O

**OTA (Over-The-Air)**: Updating device software wirelessly through Bluetooth, without needing physical access to the device.

## P

**Pairing**: The process of establishing a trusted connection between your phone and datalogger device. Usually happens automatically when you connect.

**Permissions**: Approvals you give the app to access phone features like Bluetooth, location, and file storage.

## R

**Reboot**: To restart a device. Datalogger devices automatically reboot after firmware updates.

**Reconnect**: To connect again to a device you've previously connected to.

**RSSI (Received Signal Strength Indicator)**: A number showing how strong the Bluetooth signal is between your phone and device. Higher numbers mean better connection.

## S

**Scan**: To search for nearby Bluetooth devices. The app scans for datalogger devices when you start scanning.

**Send Interval**: How often your datalogger sends data to the server. Can be set from 1 hour to 24 hours.

**Server Address**: The internet location where your datalogger sends its collected data.

**Server Port**: A number that specifies which service on the server to connect to (usually 80 for regular internet or 443 for secure connections).

**Signal Strength**: How good the Bluetooth connection is. Measured in dBm, with numbers closer to 0 being stronger.

## T

**Toast Message**: A small notification that appears briefly at the bottom of your screen. Shows success messages, warnings, or errors.

## U

**Upload**: To send data from your phone to the datalogger device. Used during firmware updates.

## W

**Wi-Fi**: Wireless internet connection. Not directly used by the app, but may interfere with Bluetooth connections.

---

*Note: This glossary explains terms as they relate to the ADARKO Datalogger app. Technical definitions may vary in other contexts.*
