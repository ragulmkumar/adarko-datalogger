# Safety Guidelines

This section outlines important safety guidelines for using the ADARKO Datalogger app and managing datalogger devices safely.

## General Device Safety

### Physical Device Handling
- **Handle with care**: Datalogger devices contain sensitive electronic components
- **Avoid drops**: Prevent impact damage by handling gently
- **Keep dry**: Protect from water, moisture, and extreme humidity
- **Temperature control**: Operate within specified temperature ranges (-20°C to 60°C typical)

### Battery Safety
- **Use correct batteries**: Only use battery types specified in device manual
- **Proper installation**: Insert batteries with correct polarity (+/-)
- **Replace promptly**: Change batteries when low to prevent data loss
- **Dispose properly**: Recycle batteries according to local regulations
- **No mixing**: Don't mix old and new batteries in the same device

### Installation Safety
- **Secure mounting**: Mount devices firmly to prevent falls
- **Cable management**: Avoid tripping hazards with loose cables
- **Ventilation**: Ensure adequate airflow around devices
- **Accessibility**: Install devices where they can be safely reached for maintenance

## Bluetooth and Wireless Safety

### Signal Strength Awareness
- **Optimal range**: Maintain 2-5 meter distance during active operations
- **Signal monitoring**: Watch RSSI indicators for connection quality
- **Interference avoidance**: Stay clear of:
  - Microwave ovens
  - Cordless phones
  - Other Bluetooth devices
  - Wi-Fi routers
  - Power lines

### Connection Stability
- **Stationary operation**: Keep devices and phone stationary during:
  - Initial connection
  - Configuration changes
  - Firmware updates
- **No movement**: Avoid walking while connected to prevent disconnections

### Multiple Device Management
- **One at a time**: Connect to only one device at a time
- **Sequential operation**: Complete operations on one device before moving to another
- **Clear identification**: Label devices to prevent confusion

## Firmware Update Safety

### Pre-Update Preparation
- **Full battery**: Ensure device battery >80% before updates
- **Stable power**: Connect to reliable power source if available
- **Backup data**: Note current configuration before updating
- **Time allocation**: Plan for 15-30 minutes uninterrupted time

### During Update Process
- **Do not close app**: Keep ADARKO Datalogger app open and active
- **Stay connected**: Maintain Bluetooth connection throughout
- **Monitor progress**: Watch progress indicators continuously
- **No interruptions**: Avoid phone calls, notifications, or other apps
- **Stable environment**: Prevent movement of either device

### Critical Update Rules
```
🚨 NEVER close the app during firmware update
🚨 NEVER turn off device power during update
🚨 NEVER disconnect Bluetooth during transfer
🚨 NEVER use device while updating
🚨 NEVER interrupt update process
```

### Post-Update Verification
- **Wait for reboot**: Allow device to restart completely
- **Verify connection**: Reconnect and check device status
- **Test functions**: Confirm normal operation
- **Check settings**: Ensure configuration intact

## Data and Configuration Safety

### Configuration Backup
- **Document settings**: Record all device configurations
- **Screenshot capture**: Save images of configuration screens
- **Regular backup**: Update documentation after changes

### Data Transmission Safety
- **Secure servers**: Use HTTPS for data transmission
- **Network security**: Implement firewall and access controls
- **Data encryption**: Ensure end-to-end encryption where possible

### Configuration Changes
- **Test changes**: Verify settings work before deployment
- **Gradual rollout**: Change one device at a time
- **Rollback plan**: Know how to revert changes if needed

## Environmental Safety

### Operating Environment
- **Temperature range**: Operate within device specifications
- **Humidity control**: Avoid condensation and high humidity
- **Dust protection**: Keep devices clean and protected
- **Weather exposure**: Shelter outdoor devices from extreme weather

### Hazardous Locations
- **ATEX zones**: Use certified equipment in explosive atmospheres
- **Wet environments**: Use waterproof-rated devices
- **High voltage**: Maintain safe distances from electrical hazards

## Electrical Safety

### Power Sources
- **Approved supplies**: Use manufacturer-approved power adapters
- **Grounding**: Ensure proper grounding where required
- **Overload protection**: Don't exceed device power ratings
- **Cable inspection**: Check cables for damage before use

### Battery Handling
- **No short circuits**: Prevent battery terminals from touching metal
- **Storage**: Store spare batteries in cool, dry place
- **Charging**: Use approved chargers for rechargeable batteries
- **Heat avoidance**: Don't expose batteries to heat sources

## Operational Safety

### Maintenance Safety
- **Power down**: Disconnect power before maintenance
- **Qualified personnel**: Have trained technicians perform repairs
- **Documentation**: Follow manufacturer maintenance procedures
- **Tool safety**: Use appropriate tools for the job

### Emergency Procedures
- **Power disconnection**: Know how to safely disconnect devices
- **Emergency shutdown**: Understand emergency stop procedures
- **First aid**: Be aware of first aid for electric shock
- **Emergency contacts**: Keep emergency contact information handy

## Mobile Device Safety

### Phone Usage During Operation
- **Stable handling**: Hold phone securely during scanning/connection
- **Screen protection**: Use screen protectors to prevent damage
- **Battery management**: Keep phone charged during extended use
- **App focus**: Don't use other apps while connected to devices

### Driving and Operation
- **No distracted operation**: Don't use app while driving
- **Safe positioning**: Position phone stably during use
- **Emergency access**: Keep emergency functions accessible

## Data Security and Privacy
